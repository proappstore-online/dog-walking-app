# Competition Research — Dog Walking App

> Last updated: 2026-06-04  
> Sources: live web research conducted June 2026. All fee figures are current as at research date.  
> Purpose: ground truth for positioning decisions. Do not build features that erase the differentiator.

---

## 1. Market Size (Why This Space Matters)

The broader pet service market is large and accelerating.  The dog-walking app sub-segment specifically
was valued at **~$1.2 billion in 2024** and is forecast to reach **~$2.5 billion by 2033 at a 9.5% CAGR**
(Verified Market Reports). A separate estimate (MetaTech Insights) puts the dog walking *services* market
(platform + independent) at **$1.18 billion in 2024**, growing to **$3.08 billion by 2035 at 9.1% CAGR**.

Key demand driver: **over 70% of dog walking revenue is generated by independent contractors** — not
employees of platforms (WiFi Talents, Feb 2026). That's the exact audience this app serves.

## 3. Competitor Deep-Dives

### 3a. Rover (rover.com) — Market Leader

**Model:** Managed marketplace. Platform owns booking, payment, and messaging.

**Fees (2026):**
- Walkers/sitters keep **80%** — Rover takes a **20% service fee** per booking.
- RoverGO tier: walkers keep only **75%** (25% commission).
- California: 25% commission (AB5 regulations).
- Pet owners pay an **additional 11% booking fee** (capped at $50) on top of the listed rate.
- New walkers pay a **$49–$79 non-refundable profile review fee** to join.

**Scale:** The dominant player. Had **91% of US sales** between Rover and Wag in 2022.

**Strengths:** Huge brand, 24/7 support, background checks, insurance coverage, established review system.

**Walker pain points:**
- Rover collects $87–108 on a typical $350 booking — with no volume discount, ever.
- At $3,000/month income, Rover's cut totals **$7,200/year** ($36,000 over five years).
- Walker can't contact owner outside the platform; algorithm controls visibility.
- Profile suspended at Rover's discretion.

**Owner pain points:**
- The 11% "surprise fee" appears at checkout — the browse price is never the pay price.
- Cannot reach walkers directly or build an off-platform relationship.

**Gap this app exploits:** Rover is expensive and intermediating. Walkers who already have (or want) a
direct client base need a presence, not a middleman.

---

### 3b. Wag! (wagwalking.com) — On-Demand, Now in Bankruptcy

**Model:** Uber-style dispatch. Owners request a walk; Wag dispatches the nearest available walker.

**Fees (2026):**
- Wag takes **40% commission** from walker earnings — the highest in the industry.
- Additional flat **$2.99 booking fee** (or $14.99 for sitting/boarding) charged to owners.
- Optional **Wag Pro** upgrade: $149 one-time (or $129 for existing walkers) for priority placement.

**Status:** **Wag! filed for bankruptcy on 21 July 2025** due to $69.5 million in financial losses
(2022–2024). It continues operating under restructured ownership.

**Walker pain points:**
- 40% commission described by workers as "exploitative and unfair."
- Experienced walkers who have options tend to leave for better-paying platforms — high commission
  drives *adverse selection*, attracting lower-skill workers.
- On-demand model: owners rarely get the same walker twice; no long-term client relationships.
- Thin coverage outside major metro areas; on-demand requires walker density that doesn't exist everywhere.

**Gap this app exploits:** Wag is a staffing platform, not a directory. Independent walkers wanting to
build their own client base are excluded or underserved. The bankruptcy signals the 40% model is
structurally unsustainable.

---

### 3c. Care.com (care.com) — Generic Job Board

**Model:** Subscription directory. Workers list for free (basic); owners pay a premium subscription
to message caregivers and access background checks.

**Fees:**
- Walkers: basic listing free; premium membership available with enhanced features.
- Owners: **premium subscription required** to message walkers and request background checks.
- Care.com does **not** take a booking commission — walkers keep 100% of earnings.
- Average walker rate listed: **~$16.63/hr nationally** (Care.com, June 2026).

**Strengths:** Multi-category (childcare, pet care, elder care), 20+ country coverage, large user base.

**Weaknesses:**
- Double friction: owners must pay a subscription just to *contact* a walker.
- Generic, not dog-specific — competes with nannies, housekeepers, tutors for attention.
- Historical trust/data-quality concerns.
- Communication still routes through the platform (no direct phone/email on public profile).

**Gap this app exploits:** Zero cost on both sides — no subscription wall blocking contact.

---

### 3d. Pawshake (pawshake.com) — EU/AU Focused

**Model:** Commission marketplace focused on home boarding and sitting; walking is secondary.

**Fees:**
- **19% commission** from sitters/walkers on every booking.
- No separate owner booking fee (bundled into the sitter rate).

**Coverage:** Strong in Europe and Australia; growing but smaller US network.

**Strengths:** Multi-country, home-boarding focus, reasonable commission vs. Wag.

**Weaknesses:**
- Dog *walking* is not the focus — sitter pool skews toward boarding and sitting.
- Coverage varies significantly city-to-city; not globally consistent.
- All communication through the platform.
- Walkers report profile deletion without due process in dispute reviews (Trustpilot, 2026).

**Gap this app exploits:** Commission-free + genuinely global (any city can self-populate, no
platform geographic gate required).

---

### 3e. PetBacker — International, Commission-Reducing Model

**Model:** Commission marketplace available in 50+ countries.

**Fees:** Starts at **25% commission**, reduces to **15%** as the walker builds reputation.

**Coverage:** Europe, Asia, Middle East, Americas. Variable quality by region.

**Strengths:** Wide geographic reach, badge system for reputation.

**Weaknesses:** High upfront commission; US pet owners less likely to recognise the brand.

---

### 3f. Holidog — Europe-Focused, No Commission Model

**Model:** Subscription or one-time fee to sitters; no commission per booking.

**Notable:** Holidog does **not charge commission** on bookings — one of the few structured platforms
that doesn't. However it **vets providers before approving** profiles, and is **web-only** (no mobile app).

**Gap this app exploits:** Holidog is EU-focused and has no mobile presence. This app is
mobile-responsive and globally accessible from day one.

---

### 3g. Tails (trytails.com) — Premium Niche, Launched Dec 2025

**Model:** Curated premium marketplace. Providers undergo **in-person interviews** with skill
verification (leash reactivity, medication, separation anxiety). 15% total commission (10% from
provider + 5% from owner).

**Scope:** Currently Chicago-only.

**Notable:** A direct signal that the market is moving toward *lower* commission and *better vetting*,
not higher fees. But Tails is premium and geographically limited.

---

### 3h. Facebook Groups / Nextdoor / Gumtree / Craigslist

**Model:** Free social posts or free classified ads in local community spaces.

**Strengths:** Zero cost, wide community reach, trusted local feel.

**Weaknesses:**
- Posts disappear; no permanent profile.
- No structured data (rates, services, service area, credentials).
- Owner must scroll endlessly; no search or filter.
- No trust signals; no review structure.
- Walker has no persistent online presence — must re-post regularly.

**Gap this app exploits:** Permanent, searchable, structured walker profiles with filterable
city/suburb — the structure that social posts fundamentally cannot provide.

## 5. The Gap: What No Competitor Occupies

> **Structured discoverability + zero cost on both sides + direct contact + globally accessible + permanent profiles.**

The gap is real and confirmed by the fee table above:

- **Free + structured** doesn't exist (social platforms are free but unstructured; structured platforms charge).
- **Direct contact + permanent** doesn't exist at zero cost (Care.com is the closest, but locks contact behind a subscription).
- **Globally accessible without a geo-gate** is unmatched — even Rover has country-specific platforms and limited coverage outside US/EU.

The market signal that validates this gap:
- Over 70% of dog walking revenue flows through **independent contractors** who need a presence, not a managed booking platform.
- Wag's bankruptcy — driven by a 40% commission that drove walkers away — confirms that the high-fee managed model has structural limits.
- The emergence of lower-commission competitors (Tails at 15%, PetBacker reducing to 15%) shows the market is moving in this direction; this app simply takes it to its logical conclusion (0%).

## 6. Strategic Non-Negotiables (for the Build Team)

These follow directly from the competitive analysis and must never be reversed:

| Rule | Rationale |
|------|-----------|
| **Free forever for walkers** | The moment a listing fee appears, this app collapses into Pawshake/Care.com territory. |
| **Free forever for owners** | The moment a subscription appears, this becomes Care.com. |
| **No platform booking/payment** | Adding booking turns this into Rover. That's the moat-erasing move. |
| **Direct contact info on public profile** | Phone/email/Instagram visible without login — this is the core value proposition. |
| **No geo-gating** | City filter must work for any city worldwide. Restricting to specific countries would forfeit the global gap. |
| **No profile expiry** | Listings persist until the walker deactivates. Gumtree-style expiry defeats permanent presence. |

These are **not MVP scope** — take to the PO in the Build tab when ready.

| Opportunity | Competitor gap it fills |
|-------------|------------------------|
| Verified reviews | FB Groups/Gumtree have zero review structure — this closes the trust gap vs. social channels |
| "Accepting new clients" toggle | None of the free channels offer real-time availability signals |
| Insurance/certification self-declaration badge | Rover/Wag use vetting as trust signal; a lightweight badge closes the gap without a full background-check system |
| Walker availability calendar | Simple open/closed indicator — not booking, just discoverability signal |
| Owner saved/favourites list | Unmet by any free channel |
